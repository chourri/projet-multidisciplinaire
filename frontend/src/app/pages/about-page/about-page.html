Climate Extreme Prediction System:
A Neuro-Symbolic Approach

In an era defined by rapid climatic shifts, the ability to anticipate extreme weather events has moved from a scientific curiosity to a critical societal necessity. The Climate Extreme Event Prediction System (CEEPS) is not merely a forecasting tool; it is an engineering response to the urgent need for proactive disaster management in the Beni Mellal-Khenifra region.

Our Objective: To bridge the gap between raw Big Data and actionable human decision-making. By fusing the predictive power of Deep Learning (LSTM Networks) with the semantic reasoning of Knowledge Engineering, we aim to detect anomalies—Heatwaves, Droughts, and Storms—days before they threaten our infrastructure and agriculture.

This project represents the culmination of our multidisciplinary training at ENSA Khouribga, synthesizing cloud computing, algorithmic intelligence, and web technologies into a unified, resilient architecture.
